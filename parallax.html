Styracosaurus

Not a Triceratops, but it’s in the same classification! It’s a Ceratopsian, which is any herbivore in the Cretacious period with a bony frill. Unlike the Triceratops, Styracosaurus only has one pronounced horn on the end of its head. Its frill makes up for it, being covered in sharp horns!

Here’s your Triceratops!

The word “tri” means three, and how many horns does this beast have? Unlike the Styracosaurus, Triceratrops lacks horns on it's frill.

Diabloceratops

A smaller Ceratopcian standing at 2 meters tall. It's armed with 2 sharp horns that curve upwards on its head, while another set curve outwards on its frill.

Mercuriceratops

This isn't a Triceratops despite it's three horns. Look at the base of the frill, it has a protrusion. It's horns are also signifcatly smaller.

Pachyrhinosaurus

"Where's it's horns?" you may be asking. Much like Pachycephalosaurus, this Ceratopsian has a thick bonned plate down it's snout. It does have two horns lining up it's frill but those are hardly usable due to being angled.
